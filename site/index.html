Core construct

Ablation is not automatically abstention.

Removing decisive evidence can leave a broader syndrome or a probable working diagnosis. The benchmark grades the claim the remaining evidence supports—not the name of the experimental arm.

Method

Three decisions, scored separately.

A model can name the right syndrome with the wrong certainty, or remain diagnostically uncertain while correctly recognizing an emergency. The grader preserves those distinctions.

Scope

Small by design. Honest about its limits.

The 12 cases are synthetic methodology fixtures. Targets were reviewed by an evidence-based AI panel, not independently signed off by clinicians. Independent clinical review and a larger dataset are required before making clinical-performance claims.
